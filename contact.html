FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about our coordinate licensing, lens scheduling, and member features.

How do I book remote telescope observation time?

Observation times can be requested directly via our Observatory page booking form. Active researchers or educators are prioritized for night telescope slews, but amateur slots open weekly based on atmospheric clarity levels.

Can I download sky maps and coordinates for free?

Yes! All basic celestial coordinates, constellation outlines, and moon phase slider updates are open-source. For advanced spectroscopic datasets and RAW CCD files, a premium researcher profile is required.

What telescopes are currently available in the network?

We coordinate scheduler times across three main systems: the Very Large Telescope (VLT) array in Paranal, Chile; Keck Twin Domes in Mauna Kea, Hawaii; and the Gran Telescopio Canarias (GTC) in La Palma, Spain.

How do I submit observations to the public catalog?

Log in to your Stackly dashboard and upload your fits/WebP files. Our telemetry systems will verify star alignment metadata and catalog your coordinates within 48 hours.
