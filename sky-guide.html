Major Constellations

Click on the star nodes below to highlight outlines and reveal mythological facts about the coordinates.

Ursa Major (Great Bear)

7 Main Stars (Big Dipper)

One of the most recognizable northern constellations, containing the famous Big Dipper asterism. Its pointer stars point directly to Polaris, the North Star.

Best Season Spring (Northern)
Declination +55°

Orion (The Hunter)

7 Main Stars

An prominent equatorial constellation visible worldwide. Known for the three belt stars, the bright red supergiant Betelgeuse, and the brilliant blue Rigel.

Best Season Winter (Northern)
Declination +5°

Scorpius (The Scorpion)

18 Main Stars

A magnificent southern constellation representing a scorpion. Anchored by the massive red supergiant Antares, often called the "Heart of the Scorpion".

Best Season Summer (Northern)
Declination -26°

Stargazing Conditions

Tonight's Planet Visibility

A real-time check of sky clarity and altitudes for observing solar planets.

Mercury

Poor Visibility

Too close to the sun. Sets 30 minutes after sunset, low on the horizon.

Venus

Excellent

Visible as the brilliant "Evening Star" in the western sky for 3 hours.

Mars

Good

Rises around midnight. Visible in Aries, orange glow easily identified.

Jupiter

Excellent

Visible all night. High altitude transit, Moons easily resolved in binoculars.

Saturn

Good

Visible in early morning hours. Ring plane tilted at 3.5 degrees.
